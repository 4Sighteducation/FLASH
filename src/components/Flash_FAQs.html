Finding Topics

You have two options:

  • Use the search bar to type any topic — our smart search understands what you mean, not just what you type (so "plant energy" will find photosynthesis)

Creating Flashcards

FL4SH gives you three ways to create cards:

AI Generated Find a topic and let AI create exam-specific flashcards directly from your curriculum. Fast, accurate, and guaranteed to be on-spec.
Create Manually Write your own custom flashcards for any topic. This is one of the most effective revision techniques because the act of creating cards is itself a form of learning.
From Image Take a photo of your notes, textbook, or any study material and FL4SH will generate flashcards from it using AI. Perfect for turning class notes into study cards in seconds.

Here's a revision hack backed by science: creating flashcards is almost as valuable as studying them.

When you write your own questions and answers, you're forcing your brain to process and reorganise information — which strengthens memory before you even start revising. It's called the "generation effect" and it's seriously underrated.

  • Use AI Generated when: You want quick, reliable cards that are guaranteed to match your exam spec. Great for covering a topic comprehensively without missing anything.
  • Use Create Manually when: You want to reinforce learning by writing cards yourself, or you have specific questions you know you struggle with.
  • Use From Image when: You've got handwritten notes, textbook pages, or class worksheets you want to turn into flashcards quickly.

FL4SH offers four card types:

Multiple Choice Quick-fire questions with automatic marking. Perfect for fast revision and spaced repetition practice.
Short Answer You type or recall the answer, then mark yourself correct or incorrect. Great for testing real recall.
Essay Focuses on structure and key points for longer exam answers. You can also use voice answers for AI feedback.
Acronym Memory hooks and mnemonics to help tricky information stick.

Studying & Spaced Repetition

FL4SH uses the Leitner system — a proven spaced repetition method. Cards move through five boxes based on whether you answer correctly or incorrectly. This ensures you see difficult cards more often and mastered cards less frequently, which is scientifically proven to improve long-term memory.

Box 1 — Everyday All new cards start here. You'll see these daily.
Box 2 — Every Other Day Cards you've answered correctly once.
Box 3 — Every 3 Days Getting more familiar now.
Box 4 — Weekly You know these pretty well.
Box 5 — Retired You've mastered these. They'll pop up occasionally just to keep you sharp.

It goes straight back to Box 1, no matter which box it was in. Sounds harsh, but this is exactly what makes spaced repetition effective — it identifies gaps in your knowledge and makes sure you address them.

Study mode is the real deal — your answers affect which box cards move to, and it follows the spaced repetition schedule.

Practice mode lets you preview and rehearse cards before they're officially due, with no consequences. Think of it as a warm-up.

FL4SH has five difficulty settings you can change in your profile:

Safe Mode No timer, no shuffle. Training wheels for building confidence. XP x1.
Standard Shuffle on, timer off. The default balanced experience. XP x1.1.
Turbo 30-second timer. Things are heating up. XP x1.5.
Overdrive 15-second timer. For the ambitious. XP x2.
Beast Mode 5 seconds. No mercy, no hints, no excuses. XP x3.

Voice Answers

For Short Answer and Essay cards, you can tap the microphone button and speak your answer out loud. FL4SH will analyse what you said and give you feedback on what you got right and what you missed.

Speaking answers out loud is one of the most effective revision techniques — and one most students never use.

It forces active recall (rather than passive recognition), helps identify gaps in your understanding, and mimics exam conditions where you need to explain things in your own words. Plus, you get AI feedback like having a tutor in your pocket.

Voice analysis requires an internet connection to process your answer and provide feedback.

Managing Cards & Priorities

The Manage section is your revision command centre. Get there by tapping any topic with cards on your homepage, or use the "Manage Cards" button in Quick Actions.

You can assign a priority (1-4) to each topic. This helps you focus on what matters most. Your homepage can be sorted by priority so urgent topics stay front and centre.

Here's a revision hack most students ignore:

Priority 1 should be your weakest topics — the ones you dislike or find hardest. Working on weak spots rather than staying comfortable with topics you already know is where the biggest gains happen.

Absolutely. Every couple of weeks, revisit your priorities. Topics you've improved on? Move them down. New weak spots appearing? Bump them up. Your revision should evolve as you improve.

XP & Gamification

XP (experience points) is FL4SH's way of rewarding your revision efforts. You earn XP by studying cards, completing the walkthrough, and hitting milestones. Higher difficulty modes earn more XP per card.

It depends on your difficulty mode:

  • Safe Mode: XP x1
  • Standard: XP x1.1
  • Turbo: XP x1.5
  • Overdrive: XP x2
  • Beast Mode: XP x3

Cards in higher boxes also earn more XP when answered correctly.
